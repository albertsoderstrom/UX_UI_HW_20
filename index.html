U.S. Department of Interior

Website re-design

TImeline: 5 weeks Role:UX/UI Designer Deliverables: Information Architecture, UI Style Guide, Wireframes, Responsive High-fidelity Mockups Tools: Adobe XD, Miro, Whimsical, Optimal Workshop, SimilarWeb

What is the Department of Interior?

The Department of the Interior (DOI) conserves and manages the Nation’s natural resources and cultural heritage for the benefit and enjoyment of the American people, provides scientific and other information about natural resources and natural hazards to address societal challenges, creates opportunities for the American people, and honors the Nation’s trust responsibilities or special commitments to American Indians, Alaska Natives, and affiliated island communities to help them prosper.

Assessing the Current Website

The data collected shows that the most regular user for doi.gov are the employees of the same department and the subdivisions of it.

Following that path, the Wireflow and Heuristic Evaluation revolves around a few tasks important for the employees. This, and further usability tests reveal an inconsistent navigation system that drove users to points without return, changing the principal menu constantly as they went deeper into the site. Mobile navigation proved even more challenging with oversized icons, long lists of links and repeated sections that made the experience more complicated and unpleasant.

Information Architecture

In order to improve the navigation system, the categories were reviewed and rearranged using Card Sorting techniques. The goal was to simplify the navigation logic, clarify the section names, and unify the employee's resources for a easy and improved experience.

Style Guide

Pictures of nature serve as inspiration for the color palette. The gray tones as primaries serve to keep a formal character adding the green and blue as accents for a feeling of prosperity and nature.

Navigations Systems

After testing early versions of the navigation, it was clear that three levels of navigation were necessary to speed up the process of finding information.
